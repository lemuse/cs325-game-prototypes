  • Originality: What is original about your game?

    This is a puzzle-based game that takes place entirely via text I/O and player-choice path selection. By making the objective collecting various objects which can be found only in specific locations, the player can explore the world and exercise their mind with word puzzles, riddles, and puns.

  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?

    Player choices initiate changes in variable state, whether that be the color scheme based on the in-game location, the variable values of in-game collectibles, or whether certain options are available.

  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?

    I learned more about constructing a non-linear storyline with conditional branching, collectibles, and I/O. The most difficult part was learning Twine's pseudo-language and applying it to specific tasks that are far simpler to implement in a more advanced coding language.

  • High Concept: A one-sentence summary of your game.

    You are exploring an Alice in Wonderland-esque world when you are challenged to gather the materials for a tea party, but in order to get those ingredients, you must undergo a series of trials.

  • Theme: State which of the Storymatic themes you used.

    I used teapot, though "making a sandwich" was originally also going to be included.

  • Mandated Variety: State which input, randomness, genre, and play style you used in this prototype.

    Input: keyboard and mouse. Randomness: random number generation. Genre: puzzle, text-based, choose-your-own-adventure. Play style: point and click, word/phrase typing.

  • Prototype Goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating?

    Implementation of theme changes, variable-tracking, and goal-achievement.

  • Player Experience Goals: What experience do you want players to have when playing your game?

    Anyone who enjoys the universe of Alice in Wonderland will enjoy the whimsey of the strange characters. People who like riddles and word puzzles will enjoy the puzzles. People who like reading will be immersed in the story more fully than if it were simple text.

    Players will attempt to go through each available story option in order to unlock as many of the objects as possible. However, not every path gives objects and certain paths give more than one object.
