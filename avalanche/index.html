  • Originality: What is original about your game?

    It's a text-based adventure with random-chance generation. There are periods in the game where sheer randomness determine's the player's fate. This was once again made with Twine, though I have since downloaded the desktop version and am, therefore, able to save my work. (And, in the future, add sound effects and pictures.)

  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?

    The incorporation of randomness and the game's tracking and return of variables at specific points during gameplay - such as whether it's day or night, which day/night it is, and how many meals you have left.

  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?

    I learned more about using Twine - namely how the conditional branching, randomness, and variables can be utilized to create a more "organic" game.

    What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?

    Writing the story itself.

  • High Concept: A one-sentence summary of your game.

    You're a veteran mountain climber trying out a new mountain without your usual climbing partner. Because climbing in real life is a careful balance of luck and skill, sometimes you can get very unlucky.

  • Theme: State which of the Storymatic themes you used.

    Gambler (ie. the aspect of randomness) and ice (ie. the setting)

  • Mandated Variety: State which input, randomness, genre, and play style you used in this prototype.

    Input is entirely click-based. Genre is text-based choose your own adventure.

  • Prototype Goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating?

    This game evaluates the gameplay and technical prowess with added variables and branching when compared with earlier iterations (such as Stargazer from last week).

  • Player Experience Goals: What experience do you want players to have when playing your game?

    I want players to be relaxed because the gameplay is really simple, but also engaged in the story since it acts like an interactive novel. The ending should be unpredictable because of the elements of randomness, and players cannot plan to counteract specific outcomes because of this.
